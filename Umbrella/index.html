Deep Dive: Who Needs Umbrella Insurance?

It's Not Just for the "Wealthy"

Many people believe umbrella insurance is only for millionaires. However, if you own a home, have a retirement account, or future earnings potential, you are a target for a lawsuit. A serious car accident or a slip-and-fall on your property can easily lead to a judgment in the hundreds of thousands or millions. An umbrella policy protects your current assets and your future income from being garnished.

Common Scenarios for Umbrella Claims

  • Multi-Vehicle Accidents: High-speed collisions or incidents involving multiple injured parties.
  • Social Hosting Liability: Accidents that occur at your home during a party or gathering.
  • Dog Bites: Even friendly pets can occasionally cause injuries that lead to expensive medical and legal bills.
  • Teen Drivers: Households with youthful drivers have a significantly higher risk of catastrophic liability claims.

California-Specific Risks

California courts often award high damages for pain and suffering. We help you calculate the appropriate limit to ensure your family is truly protected against the specific legal climate of our state.

Umbrella FAQs

How much does it cost?

Umbrella insurance is surprisingly affordable, often costing as little as $15-$30 per month for $1 million in coverage.

Do I need it if I don't own a home?

Yes. If you have a car or a job, you have assets and income that could be at risk in a liability lawsuit.

Can I get it through a different carrier?

While most carriers prefer to bundle, we have specialized markets that can write "standalone" umbrella policies over other carriers.
