Deep Dive: Why Your Home Policy Isn't Enough for Jewelry

The Danger of "Sub-Limits"

Most California homeowners policies have a "Special Limit of Liability" for jewelry theft, usually ranging from $1,000 to $2,500 for *all* items combined. If your $10,000 engagement ring is stolen, your standard policy will only pay the sub-limit, leaving you with a $7,500+ loss. By "scheduling" the item on a specialized policy, you remove these limits entirely.

Coverage for the Unexplainable

Standard policies typically only cover "named perils" like fire or theft. But what if your diamond falls out of its setting while you're gardening? Or what if you leave your watch in a hotel room? This is called Mysterious Disappearance, and it is a core feature of our specialized jewelry insurance that standard policies almost never include.

The Value of Worldwide Protection

Whether you're on a honeymoon in Italy or a business trip in Tokyo, your scheduled items are protected against theft, loss, and damage across the globe. You can wear your finest pieces with confidence, knowing you have a worldwide safety net.

Jewelry FAQs

Do I need a new appraisal?

Carriers typically require an appraisal dated within the last 18-24 months for high-value items ($5,000+). We can help you find a local certified appraiser.

Does it cover chipped stones?

Yes. Our premium policies include "breakage" coverage, which pays for the repair or replacement of stones that are chipped or cracked during normal wear.

Can I use my own jeweler?

Absolutely. We understand the trust involved in jewelry repair. You have the right to choose the craftsman who works on your items.
