Deep Dive: Dwelling Insurance vs. Homeowners Insurance

Why You Can't Use a Standard Homeowners Policy

A standard Homeowners policy (HO-3) is designed for owner-occupied residences. If you move out and rent the home to someone else without updating your insurance, a future claim could be denied because the property's use has changed. Dwelling Fire Insurance is specifically built for non-owner-occupied properties, ensuring you have the right legal and physical protection for a rental scenario.

The Importance of Fair Rental Value

If a fire damages your rental property and the tenants have to move out, you don't just lose the building—you lose your monthly income. Fair Rental Value coverage steps in to pay you the equivalent of that rent while the home is being repaired, ensuring your mortgage and taxes are still covered.

Liability Protection for Landlords

As a property owner, you are legally responsible for maintaining a safe environment. If a tenant slips on a loose floorboard or a guest is injured by a faulty gate, you could face a massive lawsuit. Landlord liability coverage pays for your legal defense and settlement costs up to your policy limits.

Dwelling Insurance FAQs

Does it cover my tenant's belongings?

No. Your policy only covers the building you own. We strongly recommend requiring your tenants to purchase their own Renters Insurance to protect their personal property.

What happens if my property is vacant?

Most dwelling policies have restrictions on homes vacant for 30-60 days. If your property is between tenants for a long period, let us know so we can add a Vacancy Endorsement.

Is earthquake coverage included?

No. Like most property insurance in California, earthquake damage is excluded. We can provide a separate quote for a California Earthquake Authority (CEA) policy if desired.
