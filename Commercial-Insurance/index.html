Deep Dive: Why Your Business Needs Specialized Commercial Auto

The Risk of Using Personal Auto for Business

Many small business owners mistakenly believe their personal auto insurance covers them while making deliveries or visiting clients. However, most personal policies explicitly exclude business usage. If you're in an accident while working, your claim could be denied entirely. Commercial Auto Insurance is designed to handle the higher liability limits and specialized usage common in business operations.

Understanding Hired & Non-Owned Auto (HNOA)

If your employees use their own cars to run errands for the business, you could be held liable for an accident they cause. HNOA insurance provides the secondary liability protection your company needs in these scenarios. It's a low-cost addition that saves you from high-cost legal battles.

Meeting California Contract Requirements

To win contracts with government agencies or large corporations in California, you'll often need to prove specific coverage limits (typically $1M+). We ensure your policy meets these "standard" industry requirements so you never miss an opportunity.

Commercial Insurance FAQs

How much does commercial auto cost?

Premiums vary based on the number of vehicles, the type of driving (local vs long distance), and the driving records of your employees. We find the most competitive rates from 30+ carriers.

Does it cover my equipment inside the vehicle?

Standard auto insurance does not. You will likely need an Inland Marine floater to protect tools, equipment, or high-value cargo during transit.

Can I get coverage for a single vehicle?

Yes. Commercial auto policies can range from a single vehicle to a fleet of hundreds. We scale our solutions to match your business size.
