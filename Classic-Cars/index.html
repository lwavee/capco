Deep Dive: Classic Car Insurance vs. Standard Auto

The Problem with "Actual Cash Value"

If you insure your classic on a standard auto policy, the insurance company will treat it like a used car. If it's totaled, they will look at "Actual Cash Value" (ACV), which includes heavy depreciation. For a rare collectible, this could mean receiving a check for thousands less than the car is actually worth. Agreed Value is the only way to protect a true collectible.

Flexible Mileage & Usage

Classic car policies are significantly cheaper than standard auto because we know you don't drive your collectible every day. We offer flexible tiers (2,500, 5,000, or 10,000 miles per year) to ensure you're only paying for the time you're actually on the road.

The "Flatbed" Requirement

Standard roadside assistance often sends a "sling" tow truck that can damage the chrome, bumper, or suspension of a vintage car. Our classic car policies mandate Flatbed Towing for every assistance call, ensuring your legend is handled with the care it deserves.

Classic Car FAQs

Can I use it as a daily driver?

Typically, no. Classic policies require you to have a separate "daily driver" vehicle and that the classic is stored in a locked garage.

Are modifications covered?

Yes! We have specialized endorsements for "pro-touring" and custom builds, ensuring your aftermarket investments are fully protected.

Do I need an appraisal?

For most vehicles under $50,000, we can often agree on a value using market data. High-value exotics or rare antiques may require a professional appraisal.
