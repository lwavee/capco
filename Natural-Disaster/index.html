Deep Dive: Why Your Home Insurance Isn’t a Disaster Policy

The Earthquake Exclusion

It’s a common misconception that earthquake damage is covered under standard home insurance. In reality, nearly every standard policy in California explicitly excludes "earth movement." If the "Big One" hits, you could be left with a mortgage on a home that no longer exists. A specialized earthquake policy is the only way to protect your equity.

Flood Insurance: Not Just for the Coast

More than 20% of flood claims occur in areas considered "low-to-moderate" risk. In California, localized flash flooding and mudslides can happen anywhere after a heavy storm. Flood insurance is often very affordable for inland properties, providing high-value protection for a low annual premium.

Wildfire: The Changing Insurance Landscape

Many major carriers are exiting the California market due to wildfire risk. We specialize in finding "non-admitted" markets and surplus lines that still provide full replacement cost coverage for homes in fire-threat areas. We also help you implement "defensible space" strategies to qualify for lower rates.

Natural Disaster FAQs

Is there a waiting period?

Yes. Flood policies typically have a 30-day waiting period before they become effective. Earthquake policies cannot be bound once a "moratorium" is declared after a nearby event.

What is a CEA policy?

The California Earthquake Authority is a state-managed, privately funded organization that provides most residential earthquake insurance in CA. We are licensed CEA agents.

Does it cover mudflows?

Yes. While "landslide" usually requires a DIC policy, mudflows caused by heavy rain are typically covered under a standard Flood Insurance policy.
