Deep Dive: Everything You Need to Know About Renters Insurance

It's More Than Just "Stuff"

While protecting your furniture and electronics is important, the most critical part of renters insurance is **Liability**. If a guest slips and falls in your apartment, or if you accidentally start a kitchen fire that damages the building, you could be held responsible for hundreds of thousands of dollars in damages. Renters insurance provides the legal defense and settlement costs you need to protect your future earnings.

Replacement Cost vs. Actual Cash Value

At Capital & Co, we primarily offer **Replacement Cost** coverage. This means if your 3-year-old laptop is stolen, the insurance company pays you the amount it costs to buy a *new* one today, rather than its depreciated "used" value. This ensures you can actually replace what you lost without dipping into your savings.

Meeting Landlord Requirements

Most professional property management companies in California now require tenants to carry at least $100,000 in liability insurance. We ensure your policy meets these standard requirements so your lease application is processed without delays.

Renters FAQs

Does it cover my roommate?

Typically, no. It's best for each roommate to have their own individual policy to ensure full coverage and avoid liability conflicts.

Is earthquake or flood included?

Standard renters insurance excludes earthquake and flood. However, these can often be added as specialized endorsements for a very low cost.

What if I'm a student?

Many students living in dorms may be covered by their parents' homeowners policy. However, if you're renting off-campus, you almost certainly need your own renters policy.
