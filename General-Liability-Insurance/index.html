What is General Liability Insurance?

Often referred to as commercial general liability, this insurance provides coverage for claims of bodily injury, property damage, and personal or advertising injury caused by your business operations. Whether a customer slips at your office or a technician accidentally damages a client's property, GLI ensures that legal fees, medical expenses, and settlements are covered.

Why Every California Contractor Needs a GL Policy

For contractors in trades like roofing, plumbing, and electrical work, having a robust General Liability policy is often a legal or contractual requirement. It not only fulfills state licensing mandates but also proves to your clients that you are a professional, responsible business partner. General Liability FAQ

Is GL insurance required in California?

While not always legally mandated by the state, most clients, landlords, and contracts will require proof of coverage before doing business with you.

How much does GL insurance cost?

Premiums vary based on your industry, revenue, and location. Many small businesses can find coverage for as little as $40-$100 per month.

Does GL cover my employees?

General Liability covers third-party injuries. For employee injuries, you will need Workers' Compensation Insurance.
